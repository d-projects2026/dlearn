Prompt Engineering: How to Talk to AI

We asked ChatGPT to help with a contract clause. Weak prompt: 'Analyze this contract.' Bad answer. Specific prompt: 'List potential risks in this clause, specific to MENA construction, considering Egyptian law and FIDIC precedent.' Excellent answer. This is the story of why how you ask matters.

The difference between a useless answer and a brilliant one is how you ask the question.

⏱ 8 min read✓ Free Article

Prompts: The Interface Between You and AI

An AI model is powerful but passive. It responds to prompts. A good prompt is clear, specific, and contextual. A bad prompt is vague, generic, and assumptive. Think of it like asking a person for advice: 'What should I do?' is vague. 'I have a delay on the critical path due to material supply — how should I respond?' is specific. The specific question gets a useful answer.

The Anatomy of a Strong Prompt

1

Role

Tell the AI what role to play: 'You are an experienced construction project manager with 20 years in MENA.' This shapes the perspective of the answer.

2

Context

Give background: 'We're building a residential tower in Cairo. The timeline is 24 months. We're 6 months in, on schedule, within budget.' This prevents the AI giving generic advice that doesn't fit your situation.

3

Task

State what you want: 'Analyze the risks in this schedule and recommend mitigation strategies.' Clear task = focused answer.

4

Format

Specify how you want the answer: 'Provide a list of 5 risks with probability, impact, and mitigation for each.' This prevents rambling answers.

💡 KEY TAKEAWAY

Template: 'You are [role]. Context: [background]. Task: [what you want]. Format: [how you want it]. Here's [relevant info].' This four-part structure applies to any prompt.

Weak vs Strong Prompt Examples

1

Weak: 'How do I manage risk?'

Generic answer: identify, analyze, monitor, respond. Applies to any project. Not useful. Too general.

2

Strong: 'I'm a construction PM on a high-rise tower in Cairo. We've identified supply chain risk for steel — there's been a 3-month lead time increase. What specific mitigation steps should I take given the current Egyptian import environment and our 4-month window before we need delivery?'

Specific answer addressing your exact situation: contact suppliers now, explore local fabrication, negotiate long-lead orders, etc. Actionable. Useful.

3

Weak: 'Draft a contract clause'

Generic output. Might not fit your needs. You'll need to rewrite it anyway.

4

Strong: 'Draft a delay clause for an EPC contract under FIDIC Red Book 2017 standard, but modified for MENA context. Consider: force majeure events specific to Egypt, notice requirements, acceptable extension mechanisms, and cost adjustments. Format: a 300-word clause with clear conditions and remedies.'

Specific output addressing your exact context. Much more likely to be usable.

💡 KEY TAKEAWAY

Weak prompts make work. Strong prompts save work. Invest 2 minutes in writing a good prompt. That saves 30 minutes of reworking a generic answer.

Common Construction Prompts

1

Schedule Analysis

'Analyze this schedule for risks and float allocation. We're building [project type] in [location], [duration]. Identify: critical path, unrealistic durations, sequence risks.'

2

Budget Analysis

'We're tracking these cost actuals against this budget. Analyze variance by category. Forecast final cost. Identify cost drivers and mitigation opportunities.'

3

Contract Review

'Review this contract clause under FIDIC context. Identify: risks to contractor/client, ambiguities, enforcement challenges. Suggest improvements specific to [jurisdiction].'

4

Risk Register

'Create a risk register for a [project type] in [location]. Identify: top 10 risks, probability, impact, mitigation. Prioritize by criticality.'

💡 KEY TAKEAWAY

Use these as templates. Adapt them to your project. The more specific, the better the answer.

Iterating on Answers: The Follow-up

The AI gives an answer. It's 80% useful but missing one thing: deeper analysis on cost mitigation. Don't start a new prompt. Add context: 'Great. Now dive deeper on cost mitigation — what are specific cost reduction strategies without affecting quality or timeline?' This continues the conversation with all the context already established. Much more efficient.

The Dos and Don'ts of Prompt Engineering

1

DO be specific

Vague prompts get vague answers. Specific prompts get specific answers.

2

DO give context

The more background, the better the fit of the answer.

3

DO specify format

Tell it how to structure the answer: list, narrative, table, etc.

4

DON'T assume the AI knows your industry

It might not understand MENA-specific context. Tell it explicitly.

5

DON'T trust without verification

AI makes mistakes confidently. Always verify important facts.

6

DON'T use it for decisions alone

Use it to analyze options. You decide. AI is an advisor, not a decision-maker.

💡 KEY TAKEAWAY

Treat the AI like a consultant: give them good information, ask focused questions, expect thorough analysis, then apply judgment. Never outsource judgment to the AI.
